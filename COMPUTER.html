Who invented COMPUTER

Generally people refer Charles Babbage to as the father of computer. He is an English mathematician and inventor credited with having conceived the first automatic digital computer. During the mid-1830s Babagge developed for analytical engine although it was never completed, the forerunner of the modern digital computer. The device is capable of performing any arithmetical operation, a memory unit in which stores number, sequential control and most of other basic elements of the modern computer. The memory unit was to be large enough to hold 50-digits numbers; this was larger than the storage capacity of any computer built before 1960. The machine was to be steam-driven and run by one attendant.

In 1843 Babbage's friend; mathematician Ada Lovelace translated a french paper about the Analytical Engine, she publish how it could perform a sequence of calculations, the first computer program. The Analytical Engine was forgotten until his unpublished notebooks were discovered in 1937.

Charles Babbage also invented a type of speedometer and locomotive cowcatcher.
